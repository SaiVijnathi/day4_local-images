Hyderabadi Biryani

Hyderabadi Biryani is one of the most iconic and flavorful dishes from Hyderabad, combining aromatic basmati rice with marinated meat (usually chicken or mutton) and a rich blend of spices. Known for its royal Mughlai roots and Nizami heritage, Hyderabadi biryani is famous for its distinct flavor, fragrant aroma, and the technique of slow-cooking (called dum cooking).

Ingredients:

Basmati rice and Whole spices

Saffron and Mint leaves

Meat and Ginger-garlic paste

Spices and Fried onions

Lemon juice and Ghee

Preparation:

Combine meat with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, biryani masala, lemon juice, and fried onions (birista). Marinate for a few hours or overnight for maximum flavor. Parboil the basmati rice with whole spices (bay leaves, cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, etc.) until itâ€™s about 70% cooked. Drain and set aside. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, layer the marinated meat at the bottom. Layer half-cooked rice on top of the meat. Drizzle with saffron-infused milk or water, ghee, and sprinkle fresh mint and coriander leaves. Add more fried onions for extra flavor and texture. Seal the pot with dough or a tight-fitting lid to trap steam (this is called dum cooking). Cook on a very low flame for 30-40 minutes, allowing the steam to cook the meat and finish cooking the rice. Alternatively, you can place the sealed pot in a preheated oven for slow cooking. Once cooked, gently fluff the rice, mixing it with the juicy, flavorful meat at the bottom. Hyderabadi biryani is typically served with: Mirchi ka Salan: A spicy chili and peanut curry. Raita: A cooling yogurt-based accompaniment with onions, cucumbers, or boondi. Sliced onions and lemon wedges: For added freshness and zing.
