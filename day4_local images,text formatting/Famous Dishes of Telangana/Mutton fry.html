Mutton fry

Mutton fry is a spicy, stir-fried mutton dish from Telangana. This dish is known for its bold flavors, with a spicy kick, and is often dry or semi-dry in texture. It's perfect for those who enjoy robust, fiery Telangana-style flavors.

Ingredients:

Mutton and onions

Green chillies and Ginger-garlic paste

Tomatoes and Red chilli powder

Turmeric powder and oil

Coriander powder and Garam masala

Coriander leaves and Curry leaves

Preparation:

Start by boiling or pressure-cooking the mutton with salt, turmeric, and water until tender. This process ensures the meat is soft and ready for frying. Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan and add cumin seeds. Add sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown. Add curry leaves, green chilies, and ginger-garlic paste, cooking until the raw smell disappears. Add tomatoes and cook until they soften and blend into the mixture. Add red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala, stirring to combine well. Cook the spices until the oil separates from the masala. Add the cooked mutton pieces to the pan, ensuring they're well-coated with the masala. Fry on medium to high heat, allowing the mutton to absorb the spices and get slightly crispy around the edges. Continue frying until the dish reaches a semi-dry consistency, or fry it further for a drier, crispy finish. Finish with fresh coriander leaves for added flavor.
