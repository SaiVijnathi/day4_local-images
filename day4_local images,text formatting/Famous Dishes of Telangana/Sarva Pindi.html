Sarva Pindi

Sarvapindi is a traditional snack from Telangana, known for its crispy texture and wholesome flavor. It is a savory rice flour pancake, often enjoyed as a breakfast or evening snack. The dish is also called Tapala Chekka in some regions of Telangana and is loved for its crunchy exterior and soft, spicy interior.

Ingredients:

Rice flour and Chana dal

Green chillies and Ginger

Curry leaves and Cumin seeds

Sesame seeds and onions

Turmeric and salt

Water and oil

Preparation:

Soak chana dal for about 30 minutes to soften it slightly. In a bowl, mix rice flour, soaked chana dal, chopped onions, green chilies, ginger, curry leaves, cumin seeds, sesame seeds, turmeric, and salt. Add water gradually to form a soft, pliable dough. Take a ball of the dough and flatten it on a greased pan or plate using your fingers. The pancake should be about 1/4 inch thick, and small holes should be made throughout the pancake using your fingers or a spoon to help it cook evenly. Heat oil in a pan and place the shaped dough on the pan (if shaped on a plate, invert it onto the hot pan). Cook on low to medium heat, adding a little oil around the edges and in the holes, until the pancake turns golden brown and crispy on both sides. It’s cooked slowly to ensure a crispy outer layer and a soft interior. Sarvapindi is best enjoyed hot, served with: Pickle: To add a tangy, spicy element. Coconut chutney: For a cooling contrast. Tea: It’s often paired with a hot cup of chai for a perfect snack.
