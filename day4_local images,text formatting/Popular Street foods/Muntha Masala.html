Muntha masala

Muntha Masala is a spicy and tangy street food snack from Andhra Pradesh, particularly famous in the regions of Guntur and Vijayawada. It’s a popular evening snack that combines puffed rice, a variety of chutneys, crunchy fried snacks, onions, and a range of spices. Similar to bhel puri but with a unique Andhra twist, Muntha Masala is known for its intense heat and bold flavors.

Ingredients:

Puffed rice and Fried Peanuts

Raw onions and Tomatoes

Green chilies and Coriander leaves

Lemon juice and Chaat masala

Red chili powder and Cumin powder

Tamarind chutney, Garlic Chutney and Mint-coriander chutney

salt and oil or ghee

Preparation:

Step 1: Roast the Puffed Rice Heat a pan and lightly roast the puffed rice for a few minutes on low heat to make it crispier. This step is optional but recommended for a better texture. Step 2: Prepare the Chutneys Tamarind chutney: Combine tamarind pulp with jaggery, chili powder, and cumin powder. Simmer until thick and set aside. Mint-coriander chutney: Blend fresh mint, coriander, green chilies, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt to make a spicy, green chutney. Garlic chutney: Blend garlic, red chilies, and salt for a fiery chutney (this is optional if you want extra heat). Step 3: Mix the Ingredients In a large mixing bowl, add the roasted puffed rice. Mix in chopped onions, tomatoes, green chilies, and coriander leaves. Add a handful of fried snacks like boondi, sev, or mixture for extra crunch. Pour in the tamarind chutney, mint-coriander chutney, and garlic chutney (optional) for a burst of flavors. Squeeze in lemon juice and sprinkle chaat masala, red chili powder, cumin powder, and salt. Toss everything together quickly to coat the puffed rice evenly. Step 4: Final Touches For an extra flavor boost, drizzle a little oil or ghee on top. Garnish with some more coriander leaves and, optionally, add fried peanuts, grated carrot, or cucumber for added texture. Serving: Serve Muntha Masala immediately after mixing to retain the crispness of the puffed rice and fried snacks. It’s best enjoyed as an evening snack or during casual get-togethers.
