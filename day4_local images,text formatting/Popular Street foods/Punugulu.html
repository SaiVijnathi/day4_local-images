Punugulu

Punugulu is a popular street food snack from Andhra Pradesh, particularly from the coastal regions like Vijayawada and Visakhapatnam. It is made using fermented idli or dosa batter, which is deep-fried into crispy, golden-brown fritters. Punugulu has a soft interior and a crunchy exterior, making it a beloved snack, especially during the monsoon season. Itâ€™s often served with coconut chutney or a spicy tomato chutney.

Ingredients:

Dosa or idli batter

Rice flour and Onions

Green chilies and Cumin seeds

Coriander leaves and Ginger

Salt and oil

Preparation:

If using leftover dosa or idli batter, add a little rice flour to make the batter thicker and crisper when fried. The batter should have a dropping consistency, thick but still pourable. Add flavorings: To the batter, mix in chopped onions, green chilies, cumin seeds, chopped coriander, ginger, and salt to enhance the taste. Rest the batter: Let the batter rest for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Step 2: Fry the Punugulu Heat oil: Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadai over medium-high heat. Drop spoonfuls of batter: Once the oil is hot, drop spoonfuls of the batter into the oil using a spoon or your hand. Ensure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry until golden brown: Fry the punugulu in batches, turning them occasionally to ensure they are evenly golden brown and crispy on all sides. Drain excess oil: Remove the fried punugulu and drain them on a paper towel to remove excess oil. Serving: Chutneys: Punugulu is typically served with coconut chutney, ginger chutney, or tomato chutney. Spicy powder: In some places, it's also served with a spicy podi (chutney powder) for extra flavor.
