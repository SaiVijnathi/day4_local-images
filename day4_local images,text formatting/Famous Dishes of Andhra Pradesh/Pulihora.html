Pulihora

Pulihora, also known as Puliyodarai, is a tangy and spicy rice dish that's a staple in South Indian cuisine, especially in Andhra Pradesh. It's made with tamarind, which gives it its distinctive sour flavor.

Ingredients:

Rice

Tamarind

Peanuts

hana dal and urad dal

Mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and fenugreek seeds

Curry leaves and asafoetida (hing)

Turmeric powder

Jaggery or sugar

Preparation:

Cook the rice and let it cool. Soak tamarind in water and extract the juice or use pre-made tamarind paste. Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds, chana dal, urad dal, and dried red chilies. Once they start to splutter, add curry leaves and a pinch of asafoetida. Add tamarind paste to the tempering and cook until the raw smell disappears. Add turmeric powder, salt, and a bit of jaggery or sugar if desired.
