Gongura Pachadi

Gongura Pachadi is a distinctive and flavorful chutney from Andhra Pradesh, known for its tangy and spicy taste. Gongura, also called sorrel leaves, is the star ingredient, giving the chutney its unique flavor.

Ingredients:

Gongura leaves

Green chilies

Tamarind

Onions

Garlic

Cumin seeds and mustard seeds

Oil

Preparation:

Wash and cook: The gongura leaves are washed thoroughly and then sautéed or cooked until they wilt.

Roast spices: Green chilies, garlic, and sometimes other spices are roasted or sautéed.

Grind: The cooked gongura leaves, roasted spices, and tamarind are blended into a coarse or smooth paste, depending on your preference.

Temper: Oil is heated in a pan, and cumin seeds and mustard seeds are added for tempering. This tempering is mixed into the chutney for additional flavor.

Gongura Pachadi is often enjoyed with: Rice: A common pairing, especially with a drizzle of ghee. Idli or Dosa: It works as a great accompaniment. Rotis or Parathas: For a flavorful addition to any meal.
