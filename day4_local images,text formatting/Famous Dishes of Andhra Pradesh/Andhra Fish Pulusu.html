Andhra Fish Pulusu

Andhra Fish Pulusu is a tangy, spicy fish curry that's a signature dish in Andhra Pradesh. Made with tamarind and a variety of spices, this curry has bold flavors and is typically served with steamed rice. The word "pulusu" refers to a tamarind-based gravy, which is a key element in this dish.

Ingredients:

Fish

Tamarind

Onions and tomatoes

Green chilies and red chili powder

Turmeric and coriander powder

Fenugreek seeds (methi) and mustard seeds

Curry leaves

Ginger-garlic paste

Fresh coriander leaves

Oil

Preparation:

The fish is marinated with turmeric, red chili powder, and salt, and set aside for some time. Tamarind is soaked in water, and the pulp is extracted to create a tangy base. Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds, fenugreek seeds, and curry leaves until they splutter. Add chopped onions, green chilies, and ginger-garlic paste. Saut√© until the onions turn golden brown, then add tomatoes and cook until soft. Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and the tamarind extract. Allow the mixture to simmer until the raw smell of the tamarind disappears and the oil starts to separate. Gently place the marinated fish pieces into the gravy and let it cook on low heat until the fish is fully cooked and has absorbed the flavors. Top with fresh coriander leaves. Andhra Fish Pulusu is best enjoyed with: Steamed rice: The tangy, spicy gravy pairs perfectly with plain rice. Chapati: For a more North Indian twist. Papadam or vegetable side dishes: To round out the meal.
